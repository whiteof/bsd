What is a screening mammogram?


What is it like to get a mammogram?

A screening mammogram is an x-ray that looks for breast cancer in a healthy woman who has no signs or symptoms of breast cancer.

Screening mammograms do not prevent breast cancer. Their purpose is to detect breast cancer before it is large enough to be felt or cause symptoms. If you have any breast symptoms, you should see your doctor.

A diagnostic mammogram is performed when a woman feels a lump or has breast symptoms or if her screening mammogram is abnormal.
