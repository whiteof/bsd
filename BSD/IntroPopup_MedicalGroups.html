Not All Medical Groups Agree

For women at low-to-average risk of developing breast cancer:

  • The American Congress of Obstetrics and Gynecology recommends that all women in their 40's have annual screening mammograms.
  • The American Cancer Society recommends screening mammograms every year from age 45-54, and every other year for women 55 years and older.
  • The US Preventive Services Task Force, American College of Physicians and the American Academy of Family Physicians feel that the decision to start mammograms before age 50 should be individualized based on a woman's personal risk, her values, and the potential benefits and harms of screening.
