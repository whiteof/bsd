What difference does mammography make?

While screening mammograms can't prevent breast cancer, they can reduce your chance of dying from breast cancer. The benefit of screening depends on when you start and how often you have a screening mammogram.

So What Does This Mean?

Starting screening earlier and more often can lower your chance of dying from breast cancer. However, this benefit is smaller than many people think. Whether you have mammograms every year or every other year, the difference between starting at age 40 vs. age 50 is 1 fewer death from breast cancer per 1,000 women. Whether you start having mammograms at age 40 or age 50, the difference between screening every year vs. every other year is 2 fewer deaths from breast cancer per 1,000 women.
